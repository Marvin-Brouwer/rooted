## Ingredients

### Dressing
- 2 anchovy fillets, finely minced (or 1 tsp anchovy paste)
- 1 clove garlic, minced to a paste
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 large egg yolk
- 120ml extra-virgin olive oil
- 30g Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Salad
- 2 heads romaine lettuce, washed and torn
- 80g Parmesan, shaved with a peeler
- Freshly ground black pepper

### Croutons
- 4 thick slices sourdough or ciabatta, torn into chunks
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 clove garlic, halved
- Flaky salt

## Instructions

1. **Make the croutons:** Toss torn bread with olive oil and a pinch of salt. Toast in a 200°C oven for 8–10 minutes until golden. While hot, rub with the cut garlic clove. Set aside.
2. **Make the dressing:** Whisk together anchovies, garlic paste, mustard, Worcestershire, and lemon juice. Whisk in the egg yolk. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking continuously until emulsified. Stir in the Parmesan. Season with salt and pepper.
3. **Assemble:** Toss the romaine leaves with just enough dressing to coat lightly. Add the croutons and half the shaved Parmesan. Toss again.
4. Serve immediately topped with remaining Parmesan and freshly ground pepper.
