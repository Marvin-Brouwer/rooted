## Ingredients

### Filling
- 500g cooked chicken, shredded (rotisserie works perfectly)
- 1 small white onion, finely diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp cumin
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Red Sauce
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 2 tbsp mild chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 400ml chicken stock
- 200ml tomato passata
- Salt to taste

### Assembly
- 8 small corn tortillas
- 200g mature cheddar or Monterey Jack, grated
- Sour cream, fresh coriander, and sliced spring onion to serve

## Instructions

1. **Make the sauce:** Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook for 1 minute. Add chilli powder, cumin, garlic powder, and oregano; cook 30 seconds. Gradually whisk in the stock until smooth, then add passata. Simmer 8–10 minutes until slightly thickened. Season with salt.
2. **Prepare the filling:** Sauté onion in a drizzle of oil over medium heat until softened, about 4 minutes. Add garlic, cumin, and paprika; cook 1 minute. Stir in the shredded chicken and season to taste.
3. Preheat oven to 190°C.
4. **Warm the tortillas** in a dry skillet or briefly in the microwave so they roll without cracking.
5. Spread a thin layer of sauce over the base of a baking dish. Spoon chicken filling along the centre of each tortilla, roll tightly, and place seam-side down in the dish.
6. Pour the remaining sauce over the top. Scatter the cheese evenly.
7. Bake 20–25 minutes until bubbling and the cheese is golden in spots.
8. Top with sour cream, coriander, and spring onion and serve straight from the dish.
