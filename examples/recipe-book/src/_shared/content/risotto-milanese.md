## Ingredients

- 320g arborio (or carnaroli) rice
- 1 litre hot chicken or vegetable stock
- 0.5g saffron threads
- 1 small white onion, finely diced
- 80ml dry white wine
- 60g unsalted butter, divided
- 60g Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and white pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Steep the saffron in 3 tbsp of the hot stock and set aside.
2. Melt half the butter with the olive oil in a wide, heavy-based pan over medium heat. Soften the onion without browning, about 5 minutes.
3. Add the rice and toast, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes until the edges turn translucent.
4. Pour in the wine and stir until fully absorbed.
5. Add the saffron-infused stock, then add the remaining hot stock one ladleful at a time, stirring frequently and waiting until each addition is absorbed before adding the next. This takes about 18 minutes — the rice should be al dente with a slight bite.
6. Remove from heat. Add the remaining cold butter and the Parmigiano. Beat vigorously with a wooden spoon for 1–2 minutes until the risotto is loose and creamy (this is the *mantecatura*).
7. Cover and rest for 2 minutes. Season with salt and white pepper and serve at once — risotto waits for no one.

## Notes

The *mantecatura* — the off-heat beating with cold butter and cheese — is what gives risotto its characteristic wave (*all'onda*) when the plate is tilted. Don't skip it, and don't use warm butter.
